MY INVESTMENT PHILOSOPHY

  • 01.

    Betting on Understandable Trends

    I invest in companies that align with future trends I can truly understand — businesses that deliver products or services people genuinely love and society truly needs.

  • 02.

    Price Over Quality: Always with Courage and Patience

    A great company isn't always a great investment — price matters more. I believe the perfect investment is born from patience, courage, and the willingness to go against the tide. I constantly remind myself: never invest out of FOMO.

  • 03.

    Long-Termism: Let Strategy and Value Unfold Over Time

    Great companies need time to execute strategies and turn visions into concrete results. Likewise, investments need time for value to be discovered — or rediscovered. Short-term gains or losses are just noise; I focus on the signal.

  • 04.

    Investing Is a Way of Life

    True investing goes beyond markets and tickers — it's about principles that apply to both capital and life. Even after all the analysis, your final decision often reflects your values and beliefs. In the end, how you invest is how you see the world.

WHAT I READ & FOLLOW

Memos from Howard Marks: I read every Howard Marks memo the moment it's out — always full of timeless insights on investing and thinking.
Reddit: A goldmine of first-hand opinions on products, services, and companies. I often find the most candid user feedback here — and in investment threads, sharp insights from independent thinkers.
X.com: Yes, 90% is noise — but the remaining 10% is incredibly real-time, raw, and revealing. From firsthand reactions to Tesla's FSD in China to early disappointment around Apple Intelligence, X captures the pulse.
Youtube: My go-to platform for learning new skills and exploring unfamiliar topics. I also use it to watch CEO interviews — a great way to get a feel for a company's leadership.
Zhihu.com: It's like Quora, but way more active — and uniquely valuable. Despite some censorship, you'll find Chinese AI researchers sharing raw, firsthand takes you won't get anywhere else. I've picked up a lot about LLMs here.
Xueqiu (snowball): A Chinese investment forum. I follow only one person here — Duan Yongping, a long-time value investor and a friend of Warren Buffett. From NetEase and UHAL to Apple and Moutai, his past investments are classics. He still shares thoughts and chats with users regularly.
